# Spanish Labor Law Overview

Spanish employment law is governed chiefly by the Workers' Statute (*Estatuto de los Trabajadores*, Royal Legislative Decree 2/2015), together with sectoral and company collective agreements (*convenios colectivos*). The system favours indefinite employment and provides defined severance entitlements on termination.

## Employment contracts

Following the 2021–2022 labour reform, the **indefinite contract** (*contrato indefinido*) is the general rule. **Temporary contracts** are restricted to limited circumstances — production-related needs (*por circunstancias de la producción*) and substitution (*por sustitución*) — with maximum durations; misuse leads to the contract being deemed indefinite. **Fixed-discontinuous contracts** (*fijo-discontinuo*) cover seasonal or intermittent permanent activity.

## Working time

Maximum ordinary working time is **40 hours per week** averaged over the year. Workers are entitled to a **minimum daily rest of 12 hours** between the end of one working day and the start of the next, and a **weekly rest of at least one and a half days** (commonly the weekend). Overtime (*horas extraordinarias*) is generally capped at **80 hours per year**. There is a legal duty to record daily working time.

## Paid leave and holidays

Employees are entitled to a minimum of **30 calendar days of paid annual holiday** (equivalent to roughly 22 working days). In addition there are around **14 public holidays** per year, set at national, regional, and local level.

## Severance and dismissal

Dismissal rules distinguish:

- **Objective / collective dismissal** (economic, technical, organisational, or production grounds): statutory severance of **20 days' salary per year of service**, capped at 12 monthly payments.
- **Unfair dismissal** (*despido improcedente*): the employer chooses between reinstatement or paying severance of **33 days' salary per year of service**, capped at 24 monthly payments (with transitional higher rates for service before February 2012).
- **Disciplinary dismissal** (*despido disciplinario*) for serious misconduct: if justified, no severance is owed.

## Probation and representation

Probation periods are set by collective agreement, with statutory ceilings. Workers are represented by **personnel delegates** or a **works council** (*comité de empresa*) depending on workforce size.

## Minimum wage

Spain sets an annual **interprofessional minimum wage** (*Salario Mínimo Interprofesional*, SMI) by government decree.

This summary is factual and general and is not legal advice.
